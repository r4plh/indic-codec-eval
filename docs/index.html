Indic Codec Eval

First systematic benchmark of neural audio codecs on Indian language speech

5 Languages 3 Codecs 4 Metrics 150 Audio Samples

TL;DR

We evaluated EnCodec, DAC, and SNAC on speech from Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, and Kannada. The verdict is clear: DAC delivers near-perfect reconstruction across all Indian languages. EnCodec is acceptable but degrades noticeably. SNAC fails significantly — losing speaker identity and speech intelligibility, making it unsuitable for Indian language voice AI in its current form.

Analysis

Key Findings

What the numbers tell us about deploying audio codecs for Indian language voice AI.

01

DAC generalizes remarkably well

Despite being trained on English, DAC achieves PESQ > 4.4 and speaker similarity > 0.98 across all five Indian languages. It can be used confidently in production Indic TTS pipelines.

02

SNAC is not ready for Indic deployment

SNAC loses 28% of speaker identity (similarity = 0.72) and introduces 82.9% WER on Tamil. Reconstructions are largely unintelligible to downstream ASR for Dravidian languages.

03

Hindi outperforms all other languages

Hindi consistently scores highest across all codecs and metrics. This likely reflects proximity to English phonology and higher-quality source data (48kHz studio recordings).

04

Dravidian languages suffer more

Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Bengali show consistently lower scores than Hindi. Bengali is particularly affected — SNAC speaker similarity drops to 0.632.

05

WER validates signal metrics

The codec ranking from WER (DAC > EnCodec > SNAC) exactly matches PESQ, STOI, and speaker similarity rankings, confirming these signal metrics are reliable proxies.

06

Tamil is 2x harder than Hindi

Tamil WER is 2–2.4x worse than Hindi for every codec. This reflects Tamil's agglutinative morphology, less representation in training data, and ASR model differences.

Looking Ahead

Limitations & Future Work

Limitations

  • Small sample size — 10 samples/language may not capture full speaker/domain variation
  • WER only for Hindi & Tamil — Fine-tuned Whisper models for Telugu, Bengali, and Kannada produced hallucinated outputs; WER excluded rather than reporting misleading numbers
  • Uneven source quality — Hindi at 48kHz studio vs others at 16kHz may inflate Hindi scores
  • PESQ not calibrated for Indic — Designed for English/European speech; absolute scores may not reflect native speaker perception
  • NanoCodec & Mimi not evaluated — Dependency conflicts prevented evaluating NVIDIA's FSQ codec (used in Koel-TTS) and Kyutai's Mimi
  • No phoneme-level analysis — No minimal pair tests for retroflex/dental, aspirated/unaspirated contrasts

Future Work

  • Extend WER to all languages — Fine-tune or source reliable ASR for Telugu, Bengali, Kannada
  • Add more codecs — WavTokenizer, FACodec, SpeechTokenizer, SemantiCodec, Mimi, Single-Codec, TiCodec, NanoCodec
  • Scale to 22 Indian languages — Using IndicVoices-R for comprehensive coverage
  • Minimal pair evaluation — Test retroflex vs dental, aspirated vs unaspirated, gemination
  • 8kHz telephony tests — Critical for Indian voice AI deployed over phone networks
  • Fine-tune codecs on Indic data — Measure quality gains from Indic-specific training
  • MOS listening tests — Subjective evaluation with native speakers
  • Open benchmark release — Publish as a community benchmark for Indic speech
